Pillars of "Bangladesh 2.0"

Innovation

Bangladesh 2.0 aims for innovation, digital services, startups, and inclusive tech-driven growth.

Sustainability

In Bangladesh 2.0, sustainability means using technology for green growth, equality, and resilience.

Infrastructure

In new Bangladesh, infrastructure focuses on smart cities, digital connectivity, and green development.

Empowerment

In Bangladesh 2.0, empowerment means enabling youth and citizens through innovation and technology.

The Vision of Future

Bangladesh 2.0 aims to redefine the nation’s trajectory by embracing cutting-edge technology, promoting inclusive growth, and ensuring sustainability. With initiatives in renewable energy, digital transformation, and robust infrastructure, the country is set to become a global model of development.

Digital Transformation

Creating a nationwide digital ecosystem to enhance connectivity and access to services for all citizens.

Green Energy

Investments in solar and wind energy to ensure a sustainable and eco-friendly future.

Modern Infrastructure

Building state-of-the-art infrastructure to support economic growth and global competitiveness.

"Poverty is not created by the poor. It is created by the system we have built, the institutions we designed, and the mindset we follow. The financial system was never meant for the poor—it was built to serve the rich. But I have always believed that credit is a fundamental human right. That’s why I started Grameen Bank, to give the poor—especially women—a chance to stand on their own feet. I have seen how a small loan can change a life. The poor are not helpless. They are full of potential and energy, but the system keeps them trapped. We must redesign our economy so that it works for everyone, not just a few. One day, I believe, we will put poverty in the museum. That is the world I dream of, and I invite all young people to help build it—not just by chasing profit, but by creating businesses with a purpose.”

Dr. Muhammad Yunus

Chief Adviser of the People's Republic of Bangladesh
